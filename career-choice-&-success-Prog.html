Career and Education Orientation Programme

If you have ever seen where people talk down a subject or ask why a subject is included in the school curriculum, you will know how vital this programme is.


How many careers are there in the world? Only a few schoolers in Nigeria can answer this question correctly, saying “uncountable.” Why? That is because a career advice programme is rare in our schools. When organised, the number of careers is often narrowed to six or seven, including doctor, lawyer, engineer, banker, pilot, artiste, security personnel, and businessman or woman. Most young people in underserved communities in Nigeria don't get career advice throughout their secondary school days – unless when they discuss it among themselves. How many of them know there are more careers than they would list? No wonder many young people often dream small.


As students must learn about everything in the classroom, they must also know about the job market. That will help them make connections between learning in school and a career. Many students undermine and question the inclusion of certain subjects in their curriculum because they think they are irrelevant to them since they can hardly connect the subject to any career. Most do not want to learn some things when they can’t make connections. Those connections are crucial as they help them realise the importance of learning in school and using those skills in their future career.


This aspect of our programme is designed to inform the participants how big the world is, the careers out there, and how they can get the job they want. The world is big, but our students have a narrow view of its opportunities because they don’t see beyond their communities. However, it is a big world with lots of opportunities for them to be anything they want. Through this programme, we aim to inspire the participants and make their learning meaningful by connecting the dots between their education and different careers worldwide. To show them a world full of opportunities where their choice of career can and will prosper if things are done correctly.
