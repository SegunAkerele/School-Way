School is not a scam. Education is not a scam.


Whatever happened that popularises the saying School nah scam; or Education nah scam among young people today, which necessitates the above declaration, must be an exciting event. However, the saying school nah scam has become an ideology that is sending many schoolers away from school, and the students in the school who believe in the ideology are not serious with their studies which necessitates this programme.


This is an orientation programme to address controversial and misleading ideologies, thoughts, beliefs and ideas that disvalue education in the mind of young people. The foci of the programme:


  • To help students understand the meaning of education and how to attain it.
  • To build a defensive mechanism in the mind of students against misleading thoughts that disvalue schooling in their minds by providing thoughtful answers to their controversial questions.
  • To inspire students towards self-education to be more educationally productive.
  • To keep students aware and help them understand better how to start developing soft skills besides academic grades to thrive in today’s society after graduation.
  • To adequately equip them with excellent educational experiences in higher institutions.


Our ultimate goal through this initiative is to prepare students’ minds for excellent educational activities in school, leading to exceptional educational experiences that will positively impact their lives.


This initiative is not a mere motivational program that only emphasises the need for students to read their books and be serious in school. It is a unique reorientation program for the most senior secondary school students (SS 3 students) on how to attain in school what most graduates in today’s society lack, which make them jobless and confused about what to do with their lives.
