How-to-Learn Orientation Programme

Aside from a child’s brain capacity, another excellent academic performance enabling is knowing how to learn from various sources. A child who knows how to position themselves for learning will always outsmart those who don’t know.


The three levels of education – primary, secondary, and tertiary have their different educational purposes and distinct teaching strategies, which require different learning approaches from the learners. Most students and undergraduates don’t perform well in school because they don’t know how to learn whatever they are to learn. Many children must learn to position themselves to learn and acquire knowledge in school properly.


The how-to-learn orientation is a student-centred programme that seeks to help students understand better how to acquire knowledge, learn things, and develop their talents through the school’s curricular and extra-curricular day-to-day activities.





Teaching students how to learn comes from our understanding that learning is an art, just as teaching is an art that must be mastered. If teachers have to learn the art of teaching to be effective teachers, the students must also master the art of learning to be gainful and effective learners. Not everyone is ordained as a teacher – a more significant per cent learnt the craft. Therefore, we must teach our children how to learn also.


This programme guides the participants on how to position themselves to acquire adequate and enough knowledge and develop their talents through their school’s curricular and extra-curricular day-to-day activities in a way that will lead to success before and after graduation.
