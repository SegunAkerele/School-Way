Soft skills & character development programme

It is no longer a news that a lack of transferable skills is responsible for most graduates’ unemployment. But as much as the public talks about these skills, one thing has continued to make it a problem: the question of "How students can start developing the skills early in school". The reason for such a question is that people don’t develop skills by teaching them.

However, school structured to help students develop some of the skills, but not so in an understandable way for students to easily decipher. We must help them understand the how-question.



Asking if students truly need these skills is like asking if vehicles need energy to move. They need them to thrive in the present and future society. Different organisations today charge thousands in dollors or millions in Naira to guide young people in developing these skills. People are paying that much, having learnt the hard way, to realise that their healthy personal and professional growth solely depends on the skills.

Why should schools allow their students to graduate unskilled when they can develop some of these skills in school or on campus using available means?

To guide students on how to develop some of these skills within their schools is our focus through this orientation programme.
