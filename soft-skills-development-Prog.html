It is no longer news that a lack of transferable skills is responsible for most graduates’ unemployment. But as much as the public talks about these skills, one thing has continued to make it a problem: how students can start developing them early in school. The reason for such a question is that, as we all know, people don’t develop skills by teaching them. However, school is structured to help students develop some of these skills. Meanwhile, this is not so understandable in a way that students can easily decipher. We must help them understand the how question.


We can’t start asking if students truly need these skills: they need them to thrive in the present and coming century’s society. Different kinds of organisations charge thousands and millions of Naira to guide young people who lack the skills to develop them. People are paying that much, having learnt the hard way, to realise that their healthy personal and professional growth solely depends on it. Why should schools allow their students to graduate unskilled when they can develop some skills while in school or on campus using available means?
