Responsibility Mindset Development Initiative

Why this Inintiative?

The leadership problem in Africa is little about competency but more about a sense of responsibility. Most African leaders hardly feel they are in their positions for their people, communities, and countries. But if we ask ourselves why most of these leaders lack a sense of responsibility, we can always trance the causes to their childhood.

One thing history has taught us about leaders is that no one can stop a potential young leader from becoming a leader in their future circles. Nothing else aside from death – not poverty or ignorance or illiteracy. The worst is that they will become irresponsible leaders who fail at school, in relationships, in careers, and leadership positions.

No one intentionally learns to be irresponsible. However, many people grow to become irresponsible because the process is very subtle that it has become so rampant among many students. Evidence of irresponsibility among students today includes avoiding copying notes, not studying to write tests or exams, waiting for another person to do something, feeling punished when asked to do something, etc. These are acts of irresponsibility in the making. Such children will grow up to become irresponsible leaders.

A sense of responsibility is not a virtue that can easily be inculcated overnight, especially by adults. An adult who did not grow up taking selfless responsible actions as a child or teenager would hardly take a step towards solving common problems as a community leader. That is why children in school must be intentionally equipped with the right mindset of being responsible leaders in their communities and environments since mere being in school is no longer enough to help children grow appropriately into responsible adults.

Complementing school’s effort in that regard is the main essence of ReMDI.





The Initiative

Responsibility Mindset Development Initiative (ReMDI) is a project-based learning platform where students are engaged to develop skills, such as a problem-solving skill, critical thinking skill, etc. and a sense of responsibility toward their personal growth, families, and the development of their communities.

In schools, ReMDI is established as a School Club for students, functioning as a learning hub where the members are engaged in discussions on how to attain academic excellence, develop soft and leadership skills as well as a positive character, are encouraged to dream big, are provided with leadership books, playing educative games, such as Legacy that helps them envision a future with or without adequate knowledge acquisition, and participate in voluntary social activities of the club within the school compound through which they develop leadership and other soft skills.

The objectives of the initiative are to:

  • Guide students into becoming responsible adults;
  • Prepare students to become influential positive impact makers in their communities;
  • Motivate students towards intensive knowledge acquisition and self-development;
  • Encourage students to make better career choices, among many others.
  • Guide students to develop essential soft and leadership skills necessary to achieve their big dreams as responsible young people.

We want to see young people who will genuinely take responsibility for Self-development and positive growth for the well-being of their families and the development of their communities.




The initiative accommodates both male and female students of secondary schools as members of the club without discrimination in any form. Its membership is open to all the students of the host school.

However, the initiative's members are grouped into full and participating members. A participating member is every interested student of the host school. In contrast, full members are a few elected students among the interested students in particular positions of responsibility in the club. These selected students will act as the coordinators of the club’s activities under the supervision of whomever the school chooses as the director of the club among the teachers. They are the initiative’s ambassadors who plan the club’s weekly activities and see to its growth.

To become a full member of the initiative, such a student must be a committed and active club member who attains distinction in English Studies, Mathematics, or Basic Science and must earn credit in at least nine other subjects.

The club's Facilitators’ Manual is prepared to help effectively facilitate the club’s activities.

Our focus is to instill in students a sense of responsibility towards self and community development while also developing leadership skills. Our effort is to see our members become citizens who respond to issues affecting people and their communities in the future.
